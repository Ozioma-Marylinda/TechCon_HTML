History of TECHCON

TECHCON, hosted by the Semiconductor Research Corporation (SRC), has long served as a premier gathering of the brightest minds in semiconductor research and development. Since its inception in the early 1980s, the event has grown to become a vital bridge between academic innovation and industry demand.

Beginnings in the 1980s

TECHCON was launched shortly after SRC's founding in 1982, during a period when the U.S. semiconductor industry faced stiff global competition. SRC was created to help unify efforts between universities and industry to accelerate progress.

Expanding Research Impact

Through the 1990s and 2000s, TECHCON became a showcase for cutting-edge research in materials, device scaling, and electronic design automation. The event featured collaborative projects funded by SRC and highlighted technologies that would go on to influence global chip production.

Modernization and Diversity

In recent years, TECHCON has embraced broader themes such as artificial intelligence, quantum computing, sustainability, and workforce development. Looking Ahead

With TECHCON 2025 already scheduled for September in Austin, Texas, the legacy continues. The conference is not only a hub for technological exchange but also a talent pipeline that helps shape the future of electronics.
